# Who Camber Core is for — ICP diagnostics

Use this to classify a company during DISCOVER and to run the "is this you" check.
Each segment has a structure signature, what's broken today, what Camber Core does,
and a concrete fit list.

---

## Segment A — PE / VC multi-entity operators (primary motion)

General partners, managing partners, and portfolio operators managing multiple
portfolio companies on separate stacks who want one interface across all of them.

**What's broken today**
- Each portco has its own HubSpot, Slack, Asana, and tools picked last quarter — no
  shared credentials, schemas, or context.
- You log into multiple stacks to answer a question a board member asked in one sentence.
- Senior operators spend their days assembling reports across systems.
- Native AI tooling has one credential set with no namespacing by entity.
- The decisions that move the portfolio get squeezed into the gaps between admin.

**What Camber Core does**
- One isolated deployment per portco.
- Principal-level multi-entity access — address any portco by name in one conversation.
- Operational diligence as a byproduct: which portcos run defined processes vs. tribal
  knowledge, which patterns recur and could be standardized.
- Clean exits — when a portco is sold, the deployment travels with it, procedures
  already documented.

**Is this you? (3+ = strong fit)**
- You operate across 3+ portfolio companies on separate stacks.
- At least one portco has 50+ employees.
- You've publicly committed to rolling out AI across the portfolio in some form.
- There's no dedicated AI infrastructure lead at the fund or the portcos.
- You want platform independence in writing, not just marketing copy.

## Segment B — Agencies (most important channel)

RevOps, marketing, creative, and beyond — work that needs deep, repeated context
across many clients, where contractor churn is constant and offboarding is messy.

**What Camber Core does**
- One deployment per client; agency principals provisioned across all of them;
  contractors scoped to their slice.
- Spin up a new client environment fast; revoke access the day a contractor leaves.
- Hand the deployment to the client cleanly when the engagement ends — credentials,
  history, skills, and institutional memory intact.

**Why it's the key channel:** every agency seat is a foothold into many client orgs,
with a clean upgrade path from agency-managed to client-owned.

**Is this you?**
- You serve many clients with overlapping but isolated workflows.
- Contractors rotate on and off client work regularly.
- Client data must never bleed between accounts.
- You want engagement handoffs to be clean, not a scramble.

## Segment C — Multi-location healthcare & vet roll-ups

DSOs, MSOs, vet groups, PT chains, behavioral-health platforms — PE-style
consolidators above many practices, each with its own inherited PMS or ops stack
that's too expensive to migrate.

**What Camber Core does**
- A single way for the platform operator to roll up clinical, financial, and
  operational metrics across every practice while leaving each practice's systems
  untouched.
- Surfaces operational patterns that recur across practices as candidates for
  portfolio-wide standardization.

**Is this you?**
- You sit above multiple practices/locations on inherited, non-uniform systems.
- Migrating everyone to one PMS is off the table.
- You need roll-up visibility without ripping out practice-level tooling.

---

## Segment D — Enterprise B2B SaaS leaders

Want governed AI rollout, observability, and platform independence in a single
organization. Same architecture without the multi-entity dimension. This is a
deployment pattern, not the headline pitch.

**Is this you?**
- One large org, many teams, AI rollout that needs governance and observability.
- You want to own what the system learns and keep platform independence.

---

## Pattern E — Not a fit (be honest early)

- **Labor-replacement buyers.** Anyone whose goal is to do the same work with fewer
  people. The platform is built to amplify a team; it will frustrate them.
- **Unusually static businesses.** Same people, tools, and workflows year over year.
  The continuous-fit value is muted; they're better served by Camber Core *without* an
  ongoing service relationship.

Recognize these early and say so graciously — see `what-we-refuse.md`.
