# Camber Core — the safe-altitude source of truth

Use this file for all Camber Core facts. Everything here is calibrated for outside
readers. If a detail isn't here, design conservatively rather than inventing it.

## One line

Camber Core is managed AI infrastructure that unifies AI tooling across tools, teams,
and entities — delivered by Inform Growth as a managed service.

## Entity isolation

One isolated deployment per entity. Each deployment holds that entity's tools,
credentials, data, and history, separate from every other entity's. Principals at the
top of the structure are provisioned into multiple deployments and can address any
entity by name in a single conversation:

- Pull the open opportunities from Entity A.
- Show the operational metrics from Entity C.
- Compare the customer churn signatures across all of them.

Employees of each entity stay scoped to their entity only. Nothing bleeds. Nothing
requires manual stitching. Nothing breaks when a new entity joins or an old one is sold.

## The managed service

Inform Growth runs Camber Core for clients as a managed service. A dedicated client
success operator is assigned to every account. The operator manages the relationship,
reviews the work, and is the judgment layer between what gets built and what ships.

Behind the operator, an internal agent fleet handles the heavy lifting under human
review — accelerating delivery beyond what a traditional managed service could match.
The agents do the execution. The humans make every decision that touches the client.

**The drafts-only line:** No agent at Inform Growth ships an action to a client system
without human review. Drafts in. Decisions out. That is the line.

## Skills are the new business IP

The most valuable thing a business owns in five years will be the library of executable
procedures that captures, in machine-readable form, exactly how it gets its work done.

Most businesses have no path to building one — SOPs are stale the day they're written,
and the real procedures live in senior people's heads and leave when they do. Camber
Core changes this by design: as the team works through the platform, every task is
declared, traced, and analyzed. When a pattern of successful execution emerges, a skill
is drafted, a human reviews it, and if approved it ships into the deployment as a
versioned procedure in the client's own repository.

> The methodology that discovers and compiles these skills stays with Inform Growth.
> The skills themselves stay with the client. Both sides win permanently.

## The continuous fit

Every business is a moving target — people join and leave, roles drift, tools get
adopted, acquisitions happen, sales motions change. The platform that was perfectly
tuned in January is mediocre by June if nobody keeps it current.

Inform Growth is the team that keeps the deployment current: new hires get their
workflows learned, new tools get integrated, new decisions get tracked, old procedures
get deprecated. The platform you have in two years fits your business in two years.

## Platform independence

The deployment runs in the client's environment. The data, integrations, and skill
library are the client's. If they leave Inform Growth, they keep everything built up to
that moment. What they stop getting is the ongoing service of keeping it current. The
platform is theirs; the continuous fit is what they pay for. The MSA says it plainly.

## The anti-pitch (what it isn't)

- **Not a chatbot.** It's infrastructure underneath the AI assistants the team already
  uses, not another window to ignore.
- **Not vendor lock-in.** The deployment runs in the client's environment; the skills
  are in the client's repository; leaving is not punitive.
- **Not a labor-replacement strategy.** It amplifies a team. A buyer who wants to cut
  headcount is the wrong fit, recognized early and turned away graciously.
- **Not a one-time install.** Standing up the platform is day one of work. Keeping it
  fit to a moving business is the work.
