# What we refuse — the five commitments (and the red lines)

These are the lines Inform Growth draws out loud. They double as the red lines for the
FIT action: if a prospect is on the wrong side of #2 or #5, say so graciously.

## 01 · We will not lock you in.

Camber Core is the client's infrastructure. It runs in their environment. The
deployment is theirs. The skill library that accumulates inside it is theirs. If they
part ways with Inform Growth, they keep the platform, the data, the integrations, the
history, and the skills — everything. The MSA says it plainly. They pay for domain
expertise in operating these systems and keeping them fit, not for access to a tool we
control.

## 02 · We will not pretend AI replaces judgment.

Agents are excellent at work that can be specified, executed, and verified. They are
not the right tool for reading a room, holding institutional memory in real time, or
weighing tradeoffs across information that exists only in a person's head. Those calls
belong to humans. The system serves those calls; it does not make them.

## 03 · We will not pool client data to train shared models.

Each entity's data lives in its deployment, in its environment, under its control.
Cross-client learning happens at the methodology level — better skill detection,
sharper integration patterns, better proposals — never by accumulating a corpus across
customers. Disclosed in the MSA; the line does not move.

## 04 · We will not deploy without instrumentation.

Infrastructure without observability is a tool. Infrastructure with observability and a
managed service on top is a system. Inform Growth delivers the second, not the first. A
prospect who wants the platform installed and walked away from is the wrong fit.

## 05 · We will not sell to companies that want to replace their people.

Camber Core amplifies a team. A buyer whose goal is to do the same work with half the
people is not the right fit — we say so, end the meeting graciously, and recommend they
look elsewhere. This is the architecture of the system, not a posture.

---

## How to apply these in the FIT action

- A buyer who leads with headcount reduction → **red line #5**, not a fit.
- A buyer who wants a one-time install and no ongoing relationship → **#4**, not a fit.
- A buyer worried about lock-in or data pooling → reassure with **#1 and #3**; these are
  selling points, not objections.
